# Thematic Analysis — CBC Teacher Preparedness in Kenya
## Synthesized from 22 Sources (2017–2025)

> **Method:** Inductive thematic coding of findings across all literature sources.  
> Themes were identified by frequency, convergence across sources, and direct relevance to the senior school research focus.

---

## Theme 1: Systemic Unpreparedness of Teachers (DOMINANT)

**Evidence strength: Very Strong | Sources: 18/22**

The most consistent finding across all literature is that Kenyan teachers were deployed to implement CBC without adequate preparation. This is not a disputed finding — it is corroborated across primary, JSS, and ECDE levels, across counties (Nairobi, Bomet, Kilifi, Kericho, Garissa, Vihiga, Kirinyaga), and across time periods (2019–2025).

**Key data points extracted:**
- 3% of educators feel adequately prepared (Mwang'ombe, 2021)
- 36% of teachers had not engaged in any retooling training (KNEC, 2024)
- 68% expected further training but had not received it (KNEC, 2024)
- TSC itself confirmed teachers are not well-equipped (Achieng' & Koros, 2023)
- Most pre-primary and Grades 1–3 teachers received no training (KNUT, 2022)

**Implication for your study:** This establishes the problem. At senior school level, where three distinct pathways demand highly specialized content and pedagogy, the preparedness deficit is expected to be even more acute.

---

## Theme 2: Poor Quality of In-Service Training (DOMINANT)

**Evidence strength: Strong | Sources: 15/22**

Where training did occur, its quality was consistently rated as inadequate. The core problem: training was designed as one-off workshops rather than sustained professional development.

**Sub-themes identified:**
- **Facilitator incompetence:** Training sessions were conducted by facilitators who were themselves not fully conversant with CBC (KNUT, 2022)
- **Brevity:** Short workshops (sometimes 1–3 days) were used to prepare teachers for a paradigm shift
- **Mismatch:** Training content did not match classroom realities or subject-specific needs
- **Cascade training failures:** Master trainers trained trainers who trained teachers — quality degraded at each step

**Quote-equivalent finding:** "Training sessions were poorly conducted and their effectiveness fell well below expectations" (KNUT, 2022)

**Implication for your study:** At Grade 10, pathway-specific training is even more specialized. A STEM teacher needs fundamentally different preparation than a Social Sciences or Arts teacher. Generic workshops will not suffice.

---

## Theme 3: Pedagogical Content Knowledge (PCK) Deficit

**Evidence strength: Moderate–Strong | Sources: 12/22**

Drawing on Shulman's (1986) PCK framework, multiple studies identified a gap between teachers' subject content knowledge and their ability to translate it into competency-based, learner-centered instruction.

**Key patterns:**
- Teachers defaulted to rote/lecture methods even under CBC (Isaboke et al., 2021)
- Knowledge of curriculum designs did not translate to classroom practice
- Deficiencies specifically in: assessment design, differentiated instruction, project-based learning (Keter & Wabuke, 2025)
- Kilifi County study (2025): interactive, practical training significantly improved PCK (p=0.000)

**Implication for your study:** Your RQ3 on self-efficacy and RQ1 on pathway readiness are both rooted in PCK theory. Shulman's framework is validated by this literature as the correct lens.

---

## Theme 4: Regional Inequity in Preparedness

**Evidence strength: Strong | Sources: 11/22**

A persistent finding is that the CBC preparedness gap is not uniform — it is significantly worse in rural, peri-urban, and arid/semi-arid regions.

**Pattern of evidence:**
- Garissa County (2025): severe constraints in training access, curriculum resources, infrastructure
- North Eastern Kenya: factors affecting CBC execution most severe (Muriuki, 2022)
- TSC acknowledges remote areas still face qualified teacher shortages (2025)
- 76,000 teachers deployed to JSS by 2025 — but deployment is not geographically uniform
- Incentives and intern programs used for arid areas — evidence of acknowledged gap

**Implication for your study:** County selection is critical. A study covering urban (Nairobi), peri-urban (Kiambu/Meru), and marginalized (Garissa/Turkana) will generate findings that are both nationally representative and regionally actionable.

---

## Theme 5: Head Teacher Capacity as Bottleneck

**Evidence strength: Moderate | Sources: 6/22**

Head teachers are the frontline mentors and CBC implementation supervisors. Yet:
- Only ~20% of school heads felt capable of mentoring staff in CBC (MoE, 2024)
- KEMI launched tailored training for head teachers to address this
- School-level implementation quality directly correlated with head teacher CBC literacy

**Implication for your study:** Head teachers at senior school level face a new challenge — they must now support THREE different pathways simultaneously. This is a distinct, unstudied dynamic.

---

## Theme 6: Workload and Assessment Burden

**Evidence strength: Moderate | Sources: 9/22**

CBC's Competency-Based Assessment (CBA) model requires continuous, school-based assessment records. This creates administrative burden that:
- Eats into teaching time (MoE report, 2024)
- Reduces quality of instruction as teachers focus on paperwork
- Is especially severe in large classes

**Implication for your study:** At senior school level, pathway-specific portfolios and practical assessments add an additional layer. Teacher delivery quality may be partly mediated by this workload factor.

---

## Theme 7: Stakeholder Attitudes (Underexplored)

**Evidence strength: Moderate | Sources: 7/22**

- 77% of JSS agreed that attitude/perception of education stakeholders poses a challenge (IJRISS, 2025)
- 33% of JSS teachers disagreed that attitude affects implementation
- Teacher resistance to change from 8-4-4 habits documented

**Implication for your study:** This connects to Bandura's self-efficacy theory and Fullan's change management model. A teacher who does not believe in CBC will not deliver it effectively regardless of training.
