# Gap Analysis — What the Literature Does NOT Tell Us
## The Research Space Your Study Occupies

> This document identifies the specific gaps in existing literature that justify original research at the Senior School (Grade 10–12) level.

---

## Gap 1: NO EMPIRICAL STUDY EXISTS AT SENIOR SCHOOL LEVEL ⭐ (Primary Gap)

**Evidence:** A systematic search of Google Scholar, ResearchGate, ERIC, and JSTOR using terms "CBC Kenya teacher preparedness senior school Grade 10" yields:
- Mogere & Mbatanu (2025): a *projection model* for teacher numbers — not teacher preparedness quality
- Zero qualitative or survey-based studies on Grade 10 teacher training experience

**Why this gap exists:** Grade 10 only began in January 2026. This is a real-time, emerging gap.

**What your study contributes:** The first empirical baseline study of teacher preparedness at Kenya's Senior School level.

---

## Gap 2: No Pathway-Specific Analysis

**Evidence:** All existing studies treat "CBC teachers" as a homogeneous group. No study has compared:
- STEM pathway teachers vs. Social Sciences vs. Arts & Sports Science
- Technical Studies teachers (Drawing, Electrical, Wood Technology) vs. Pure Sciences

**Why this matters:** Each pathway demands fundamentally different PCK, lab/studio resources, and assessment methods. Averaging across pathways obscures critical differences.

**What your study contributes:** First pathway-disaggregated analysis of teacher preparedness.

---

## Gap 3: Self-Efficacy Never Directly Measured at Secondary/Senior Level

**Evidence:** Bandura's self-efficacy framework is cited theoretically in 8 studies but:
- Never used as a primary measurement construct at secondary level in Kenya
- Only inferred from attitude/perception questions
- No validated self-efficacy scale used for CBC teachers in Kenya

**What your study contributes:** First application of a Teacher Self-Efficacy Scale (e.g. Tschannen-Moran & Hoy, 2001) to CBC senior school context.

---

## Gap 4: Head Teacher Support at SSS Level Unstudied

**Evidence:** Head teacher capacity is mentioned in 6 studies but always at primary/JSS level. Senior school introduces:
- Multiple pathways requiring different expertise
- External examinations (KCSE) pressures alongside CBC
- New subject combinations never taught before in Kenya

**What your study contributes:** First analysis of instructional leadership capacity specific to CBC Senior School.

---

## Gap 5: No Longitudinal or Baseline Data

**Evidence:** All existing studies are cross-sectional snapshots. No baseline measurements exist against which change can be tracked.

**What your study contributes:** A baseline dataset for 2026 that future researchers can use to track change as Grade 10 matures.
