# Annotated Bibliography
## CBC Teacher Preparedness — Kenya Senior School Research

> Organized by category. Each entry includes: full citation, methodology, key findings, and relevance to your study.

## CATEGORY A: Most Directly Relevant (Senior School / Secondary Level)

### A1. Mogere V.B. & Mbatanu (2025)
**"Projection Model of Teacher Requirements for CBC in Senior Schools in Vihiga County, 2026"**  
*African Journal of Empirical Research* | Quantitative | Vihiga County  
**Findings:** Projected teacher shortages across pathways in Vihiga senior schools; quantified gap between available teachers and CBC pathway requirements.  
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ ONLY existing study at senior school level. Focuses on numbers, not quality — your study fills the quality dimension.  
**Access:** ajernet.net

### A2. Mohamed, Ondigi & Mueni (2022)
**"Teachers' Pedagogical Preparedness for CBC in Public Secondary Schools in Kirinyaga County"**  
*IOSR-JRME, 12(01), 33–40* | Survey | Kirinyaga County  
**Findings:** Majority of respondents had not attended in-service training; teachers were struggling with CBC framework and lacked required capacity.  
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Secondary school focus — closest methodological model for your study.

## CATEGORY B: Strong JSS Evidence (Direct Baseline for SSS)

### B1. Mackatiani et al. (2023)
**"Implementation of CBC in Junior Secondary Schools in Kenya"**  
*Asia-Pacific Journal of Educational Management Research, 8(2), 35–48* | Mixed Methods | National  
**Findings:** JSS implementation challenges documented across teacher readiness, assessment, and resources. Establishes JSS baseline from which SSS departs.  
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ JSS-to-SSS bridge study.

### B2. Keter J.K. & Wabuke J.M. (2025)
**"Teacher Preparedness and Implementation of CBC in Public JSS in Bomet County"**  
*IJSSEI* | Mixed Methods | 150 teachers | Bomet County  
**Findings:** Deficiencies in pedagogical knowledge, resources, and professional development. Cronbach α = 0.82. Teacher preparedness positively associated with implementation quality.  
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Best methodological model in the literature. Use this questionnaire structure as your template.  
**Access:** researchgate.net

### B3. Cheruiyot B. (2024)
**"Challenges Faced in Implementation of CBC in Junior Schools in Kenya"**  
*East African Journal of Education Studies, 7(3), 260–266* | Qualitative | National  
**Findings:** Inadequate teacher training as critical barrier; digital tools and infrastructure shortages; rural areas disproportionately affected.  
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Qualitative depth; good thematic framework to build on.

### B4. IJRISS Study (2025)
**"Challenges in Implementation of CBC in JSS Kenya"**  
*IJRISS, Vol IX Issue I, January 2025* | Mixed Methods | 204 teachers | National  
**Findings:** 77% of JSS agreed stakeholder attitudes pose challenge; TSC confirmed inadequate teacher preparation.  
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Most recent large-sample JSS study; attitude measurement is useful for your self-efficacy dimension.

## CATEGORY C: Training Quality & Modalities

### C1. KNUT Report (2022)
**"CBC Training Effectiveness Report: Teacher Perspectives"**  
*Kenya National Union of Teachers* | Institutional Report | National  
**Findings:** Training sessions poorly conducted; facilitators themselves not fully competent; cascade training model failed; most PP1-Grade 3 teachers received no training at all.  
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Primary source for training quality data. Cite directly.

### C2. Kailo, Njagi, Kalai & Mutegi (2025)
**"Influence of Training Approaches in In-Service Teacher Training on CBC Implementation in Kilifi County"**  
*IJCSRR, 8(2)* | Mixed Methods | Kilifi County  
**Findings:** Interactivity of CBC training significantly impacts instructional delivery (p=0.000), assessment (p=0.009), and professional growth (p=0.003). Multimodal approaches recommended.  
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Direct evidence that training design matters. Key justification for your study's intervention implications.

### C3. Njiru & Odundo (2024)
**"ECDE Teacher Preparedness and Professional Development for CBC"**  
*IAJSSE, 2(3), 327–344* | Mixed Methods | National  
**Findings:** Professional development gaps persist across all educational levels; need for systematic, sustained PD emphasized.  
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ Cross-level evidence; useful for introduction section.

## CATEGORY E: Theoretical Frameworks

### E1. Shulman L.S. (1986)
**"Those Who Understand: Knowledge Growth in Teaching"**  
*Educational Researcher, 15(2), 4–14* | Conceptual  
**Core Concept:** Pedagogical Content Knowledge (PCK) — the intersection of subject matter knowledge and pedagogy. A teacher must know not only *what* to teach but *how* to teach it to specific learners.  
**Application to your study:** PCK is your primary independent variable mediating training quality and classroom delivery. Use Shulman's PCK dimensions as your questionnaire sub-constructs.

### E2. Bandura A. (1997)
**"Self-Efficacy: The Exercise of Control"**  
*W.H. Freeman* | Conceptual  
**Core Concept:** Self-efficacy — belief in one's capacity to execute behaviors to produce specific outcomes. High self-efficacy → higher effort, persistence, and resilience.  
**Application to your study:** Teacher self-efficacy in CBC delivery is your key psychological variable. Use the Teacher Sense of Efficacy Scale (Tschannen-Moran & Hoy, 2001) adapted for CBC context.

### E3. Fullan M. (2007)
**"The New Meaning of Educational Change" (4th ed.)**  
*Teachers College Press* | Conceptual  
**Core Concept:** Educational change involves three dimensions: beliefs/understanding, skills, and materials/resources. All three must align for meaningful implementation. Change is a process, not an event.  
**Application to your study:** Explains why one-off workshops fail and why sustained mentorship is needed. Frames your policy recommendations.

### E4. Darling-Hammond et al. (2019)
**"Implications for Educational Practice of the Science of Learning and Development"**  
*Applied Developmental Science, 24(2), 97–140* | Review  
**Core Concept:** Evidence-based principles for how learning environments should be designed. Validates learner-centered, competency-based approaches.  
**Application to your study:** Theoretical justification for CBC's pedagogical approach; validates the shift from 8-4-4.
