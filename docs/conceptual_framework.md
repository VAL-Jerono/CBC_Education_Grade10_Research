# Conceptual Framework
## CBC Teacher Preparedness — Senior School Kenya

---

## Theoretical Anchors

This study is gro unded in three complementary theories:

| Theory | Author | What it explains in this study |
|---|---|---|
| Pedagogical Content Knowledge (PCK) | Shulman (1986) | How training translates (or fails) into subject-specific classroom delivery |
| Self-Efficacy Theory | Bandura (1997) | Why some teachers deliver CBC effectively despite similar training |
| Change Management Theory | Fullan (2007) | Why systemic factors (policy, leadership, resources) mediate teacher behavior |

## Variable Operationalization

| Variable | Type | How Measured |
|---|---|---|
| In-service training quality | Independent | Questionnaire: frequency, duration, content relevance, pathway specificity (5-point Likert) |
| Pedagogical Content Knowledge | Mediating | Questionnaire: subject knowledge + pedagogical skill sub-scales (adapted from Shulman) |
| Teacher Self-Efficacy | Mediating | Adapted TSES (Tschannen-Moran & Hoy, 2001) — 24 items, 9-point scale |
| Classroom delivery quality | Dependent | Questionnaire (self-report) + interview (head teacher assessment) |
| Head teacher support | Moderating | Questionnaire sub-scale on mentorship and observation frequency |
| School resources | Moderating | Checklist of available CBC resources per pathway |
| Regional context | Grouping | County category: Urban / Peri-urban / Marginalized |
| Pathway type | Grouping | STEM / Social Sciences / Arts & Sports Science |
